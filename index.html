Working Papers

The Macroeconomic Effects of Gig Work: Insurance Against Labor Market Risk

Kisan Choi

[Abstract] This paper examines gig employment as a form of self-employment that provides protection against labor market risk. Using a quantitative macroeconomic model calibrated to U.S. labor statistics, I find that gig work decreases joblessness and raises traditional employment wages, yet reduces firm hiring and shifts workers toward lower-output positions. Welfare gains appear across all demographics, particularly benefiting uninsured jobless persons. However, labor reallocation causes aggregate output and productivity declines, highlighting policy trade-offs between income security and productive efficiency.

Revisiting Labor Market Policies in the Presence of Fallback Self-employment

Kisan Choi

[Abstract] This paper investigates how accessible flexible self-employment influences the effectiveness of labor market policies. I evaluate five interventions — unemployment insurance, termination restrictions, worker bargaining strength, recruitment incentives, and matching improvements. Simulations demonstrate that policy impacts depend substantially on fallback employment availability and occupational transition patterns. Flexible self-employment magnifies unemployment benefit effects, reduces firing cost consequences, and decreases active labor program success rates.

Effects of New Technology Adoption in Korea: Implications for Growth, Employment, and Inequality

Kisan Choi and Eunseong Ma

BOK Working Paper No. 2026-7

[BOK Link]

[Abstract] This paper models technology adoption interactions with labor frictions in Korea using a heterogeneous-agent general equilibrium framework. Results indicate long-run output increases but aggregate employment falls as workers reallocate toward skill development. The direction of technological change proves central to both macroeconomic outcomes and distributional consequences.
